## Design Context

### Users
B2B procurement managers, project engineers, and electrical contractors across ASEAN and the Middle East. They visit to evaluate products for infrastructure projects, check specifications, and submit enquiries. Context: office or job site, desktop-primary. They value trust, precision, and proof — not flash.

### Brand Personality
Established. Precise. Industrious. U-LI is Malaysia's largest cable support systems manufacturer, listed on Bursa Malaysia since 1983. The brand should feel like a trusted institution, not a tech startup.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial Modernism — warm, industrial, document-like. Cream/parchment base (#F5EDD6), deep ink (#1A0F00), burnt orange accent (#C4622A). American Typewriter for all headings (gives the archival, engineered-document feel). Raleway for body copy. Paper texture overlay at 15% opacity. Double-line and single-line rule dividers from the original brand assets separate sections. No gradients. No rounded cards. No glassmorphism. Monochromatic photos where possible.

### Design Principles
1. **Faithfulness over creativity** — this is a replica of an existing site. Match the reference; don't freelance new directions.
2. **Industrial precision** — square corners, strong typographic hierarchy, no decorative flourishes that aren't in the reference.
3. **Trust signals over decoration** — stats, certifications, client logos, and project photos do the heavy lifting. White space is generous.
4. **Left-aligned editorial layout** — headings default left-aligned. Centre only the hero H1.
5. **Accent is rare** — #C4622A only for orange subheadings, hover states, and the primary CTA button. Never backgrounds.
